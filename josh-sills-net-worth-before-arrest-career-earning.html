Josh Sills Early Career And Achievements

Born Joshua Matthew Sills, Josh Sills was born on 26 January 1998. The 25 years old played in Sarahsville, Ohio and went to Meadowbrook High School in Byesville, where he was a two-year starter.

The Ohio native committed to West Virginia University, where he redshirted his first year. He participated in 13 games as a freshman in 2017 and started 10 of them, five at left guard and the remaining five at right guard.

Moreover, the footballer played in 681 snaps and led his team with thirteen knockdown blocks. In 2018, Josh started 12 games, participated in 970 plays, and was later selected for second-team All-Big 12 Conference.

In 2019, he played in two games and made two starts. However, the offensive guard missed the rest of the season.

For the 2020 season, Josh Sills transferred to Oklahoma State University. He started all 11 games of the year and made it to a first-team All-Big 12 Conference pick.

In January 2021, the athlete announced that he would rejoin the team for his senior season.

Sills was elected team captain as a senior and led Oklahoma State in both knockdown blocks (41) and pancake blocks (21) to earn a spot on the first team of the Big 12 for the second year in a row.

Additionally, Joshua earned a nomination for the Big 12 Offensive Lineman of the Year award.

Josh Sills’ Accusations

Philadelphia Eagles offensive guard was accused of holding a victim while engaging in nonconsensual sexual conduct.

The alleged incident is said to have happened on 5 December 2019, according to a statement released on Wednesday by Ohio Attorney General Dave Yost and Guernsey County Sheriff Jeffrey Paden.

The footballer received a summons to court on 16 February.

On the other hand, both NFL and the Eagles released statements after the news came to knowledge. The Eagles said they knew about the legal matter and communicated with the league office.

Likewise, The NFL said the issue would be reviewed under its conduct policy. They also informed the public that they placed the offensive guard on the Commissioner Exempt list.
