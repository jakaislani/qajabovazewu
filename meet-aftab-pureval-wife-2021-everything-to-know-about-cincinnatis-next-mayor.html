Aftab Pureval, married to his wife Whitney Whitis, is the new mayor of Cincinnati. Learn everything about Cincinnati’s next mayor.

Aftab Karma Singh Pureval is an American lawyer and politician who currently serves as the clerk of the Hamilton County Courts in Ohio.

He announced his campaign for mayor of Cincinnati in the 2021 election on January 14, 2021, and was elected on November 2, 2021.

In 2018, he ran for Congress in Ohio’s 1st congressional district. In addition, he was elected as Cincinnati’s first Asian American mayor in November 2021.

Aftab received 39 percent of the vote in a primary election held on May 4, 2021, out of a field of six candidates. As a result, Pureval was elected with 66 percent of the vote over Democratic candidate David S. Mann on November 2, 2021.

Aftab Pureval Wife Whitney Whitis & Children

Aftab Pureval is married to his wife, Whitney Whitis. On April 13, 2017, they got engaged, and on May 26, 2018, they married.

Whitis is a Bethesda North Hospital hospitalist and a graduate of Ohio State University. They had a son together, and Bodhi, their son, was born in the year 2019.

He formerly resided in a home he purchased in Cincinnati’s Hyde Park area in 2015. However, he stated in January 2018 that he had lately relocated to Downtown Cincinnati.

As of 2021, Aftab lives with his wife and his son in Clifton.

Aftab Pureval Parents And Family

Aftab Pureval was born to his parents in Xenia, Ohio.

His family relocated to Beavercreek when he was four years old. Aftab’s parents were immigrants: his father, Devinder Singh Pureval, was an Indian immigrant, and his mother, Drenko, was a Tibetan refugee.

As a child, he was “awkward, quiet, distant,” according to Pureval, but as he grew older, he became more outgoing. He only has one sister, Avid, who is about six years younger than him.
