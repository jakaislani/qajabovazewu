Michele Carey: Biography, Career, Husband, Death

Michele Carey is famed for her role as Josephine “Joey” MacDonald in the movie El Dorado, but that isn’t just the only thing she was known for. She has been in other movies, playing several roles until her sad demise.

Her demise meant that America had lost one of the best actresses to have graced the screens.

In this article, we talk about the life of Michele Carey, who was one of the best until her death.

We discuss her career, early life, education and her sad demise at what many would describe as a ripe age of 75, but given her influence in the entertainment industry, she would have made more impact had she lived further than that.

Michele Carey Biography

Michele Carey Biography is so rich, as she had lived quite an accomplished life.

It is why people still look for more information about her.

The actress was born on February 26, 1942, in Annapolis, Maryland, to Stanley Willard Henson, Jr., and Thelma Burnell Henson; her father was working as a wrestling instructor at the U.S. Naval Academy.

The family soon moved to Rochester, Minnesota, where her father continued his medical studies.

Michele Carey education

With regards to Michele Carey education, a lot could be said as her father was a medical person, so it makes sense that she was given the best of education because her parents knew the essence of education.

Hence, when the family moved to Fort Collins, Colorado, where her father practised as a physician, becoming Fort Collins’ first open-heart surgeon, Michele Carey had to go to school there.

She graduated from Fort Collins High School in 1960.

Michele Carey career

With a father who was into medicine and healing people, why was Michele Carey career take the form of entertaining people?

With most parents always wanting their children to take after them and follow their career pattern, it didn’t quite turn out this way for a career as she decided that she would build a career in acting.

After graduating from high school, she was signed by the Powers agency and moved to Los Angeles in 1964 with her son to pursue a modelling career.

She enjoyed success as a model, but she was more interested in acting. Aided by her beauty and trademark long, wild hair, she soon caught the eye of Hollywood producers.

In 1964, she made her first television appearance on The Man from U.N.C.L.E. as a receptionist.

The following year, Michele Carey did more television work, had a small part in the classic beach party film How to Stuff a Wild Bikini (1965), and acted in her first major film, the 1966 Howard Hawks-produced and directed Western El Dorado, with a memorable role as high-spirited troublemaker Josephine “Joey” MacDonald, acting alongside John Wayne, Robert Mitchum, and James Caan.

Michele career television appearances

Michele career television appearances have seen her play roles in the following television series: The Man from U.N.C.L.E., Mission Impossible, It Takes a Thief, The Wild Wild West, The Night of the Feathered Fury, The Night of the Winged Terror, The F.B.I., Starsky and Hutch, and many others before her retirement–which was short-lived as she made a comeback.

Michele Carey retirement

Michele Carey retirement wasn’t well-received it would seem, as she come back to play a few roles in other movies.

Carey retired from acting in 1984 but made a brief comeback in the film In the Shadow of Kilimanjaro (1986), which was her last performance.

She also appeared as Crystal in a 1982 episode of the television series The Fall Guy.

Michele Carey marriage

Michele Carey marriage took several turns.

She was briefly married to her son’s father in 1961.

Michele Carey marriage happened again in the early 1970s to the man who would adopt her son.

She was also briefly married in the early 1990s to a businessman in New Mexico. Carey’s last marriage was with businessman Fred G. Strebel in 1999, and she resided with him in Hillsborough and Rancho Mirage.

Strebel died on December 28, 2011.

Michele Carey death

Michele Carey death came as a shock to, not just her family but to the several fans she made while playing several roles in television series and in movies.

Michele Carey death was a result of what they described as natural causes.

Michele Carey death happened on November 21, 2018, in Newport Beach, California.

Her father, who had been the oldest living NCAA wrestling champion, died earlier the same year.

Her mother died in 2016. She was preceded in death by her only child Kevin Troy Schwanke.
